Factorial Analysis

In this factorial analysis project, we explore the concept of rurality before proceeding with data analysis. Rurality, often subject to ambiguity, encompasses two main interpretations: one focusing on demographic and spatial aspects tied to agricultural production, and another emphasizing the social relationship between society and rural spaces. Our goal is to reduce variables through factorial analysis, aiming to gain deeper insights into our rural data.

The project focuses on using the Carseats dataset to predict whether stores will have high or low sales of children's car seats. The aim is to understand how different factors affect sales and make strategic decisions in the children's retail market.

Random Forest Regression

In this project, using the Boston dataset from the scikit-learn library, the aim is to predict the median housing price in the city of Boston based on socio-economic information of the neighborhood. The Random Forest regression model is employed for this purpose, using the MEDV variable as the response and all other available variables as predictors. This methodology, implemented with the RandomForestRegressor class from sklearn.ensemble, offers an effective way to understand housing price trends in relation to various socio-economic factors.

Classification Tree

In this classification tree project, the Carseats dataset is utilized, containing information about the sale of infant car seats in 400 different stores. For each of the 400 stores, 11 variables have been recorded. The objective is to generate a classification model that predicts whether a store has high sales (Sales > 8) or low sales (Sales <= 8) based on all available variables. This model will allow for identifying key factors influencing infant car seat sales and providing valuable insights for retail sales strategy.
